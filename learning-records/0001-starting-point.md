# Starting point: dabbled, lacks the "why"

Learner has hands-on exposure (blinked an LED, followed kit tutorials) but, by their own
account, does **not** understand *why* circuits work. So: comfortable with the physical
ritual of building, shaky on the mental model.

Implications for teaching:
- Don't re-teach how to plug things into a breadboard mechanically — they've done it. Do teach
  the conceptual layer underneath (what's actually happening electrically).
- Prior "exposure" is not understanding; require evidence (quiz/explanation) before treating a
  concept as known.
- Mission is concrete and motivating: build home-automation gadgets (ESP32/Arduino, WiFi, relays,
  sensors). Keep tying lessons to that. See [[MISSION.md]].
- Hard constraint: **minimal math** — intuition and rules of thumb over equations.
- Mains-voltage safety will be critical when relays arrive; keep early work at safe 3.3V/5V.

Lesson 1 (`0001-what-is-a-circuit.html`) introduced the loop + voltage/current/resistance via the
water analogy. Awaiting evidence (quiz performance / their explanation) before recording genuine
mastery of V/I/R.
