# Mission EXPANDED to a second domain: guitar effects (Lessons 23–29 added)

The learner surfaced a strong second interest — **breadboarding guitar effect pedals** (guitar → breadboard
→ amp; distortion, overdrive, delay, etc.). They explicitly confirmed they want it kept in this **same
course/workspace**, not split out. MISSION.md was broadened to two concrete goals (home automation +
guitar effects) sharing one electronics foundation. Cross-links to [[MISSION.md]].

Also: new "Guitar Effects Building Blocks" reference; glossary section for analog audio (AC signal, bias/

Key teaching notes for future sessions:
- This domain reuses prior components heavily but introduces the **AC-signal/waveform** mental model — a
  genuine conceptual shift from the DC/digital work in Lessons 1–22. Watch for confusion between coupling
  caps (signal path) and decoupling caps (power), and between a transistor as switch (L6) vs amplifier (L25).
- Lesson 29 is a **hands-on build** — the best candidate for doing *with* the learner (choose values, wire,
  troubleshoot by ear). Storage strength here comes from actually building, not reading.
- **Delivered, not demonstrated** (same caveat as records 0002–0005).
- The course now has TWO mission arcs. Future "what next?" should ask which arc the learner wants to pursue,
  or whether they're ready to build a real project in either domain.
